Netflix: A Streaming Content Giant

Made by Abhijit Chebiyam (CS 416)

The Bar Chart

Context

The content-streaming based platform known as Netflix has been very successful over the last several years due to its enhanced user experience and the plethora of options available to customers. However, Netflix's content is based around one audience group much more than any other: TV-MA.

Interactivity

Use the "Rating" and "Seasons" buttons to display different bar graphs. A mouseover will show the bar chart's corresponding axes values.

The Circle Packing Chart

Context

Many of these TV-MA rated shows are posted on the platform because it gives Netflix the ability to switch between PG-13 and R-rated content. When a program is TV-MA rated that typically means there will be one out of these three topics: cursing, graph violence, and/or nudity.

Interactivity

The circle-packing chart enables a user to view the content Netflix has generated for its audience on a global scale. The size of the circle corresponds to the content created by Netflix in that particular division.

The Line Chart

Context

TV-MA shows describes 61% of Netflix's content, while 18% is made up of TV-14 content, and only 16% of the programming is for families. As Netflix's content has tunneled into personalized content, its stock value has drastically improved over the years.

Interactivity

The line chart shows the change in Netflix's stock price over the last two decades. A user can interact with the line chart to show the cumulative number of TV-MA programs released and Netflix's corresponding stock price during that year.

Conclusion

Final Thoughts

This project was made possible through the use of D3, D3 annotations, and Observable. fullpage.js was also utilized to make a fullscreen scrolling website. Lastly, I used Python to parse the data that I extracted from Kaggle regarding Netflix shows and stock price. Through each of these resources I enjoyed making an interactive slideshow that included scenes, annotations, parameters and triggers.
